I just read the most recent number of RB monthly, and I find it very interesting.
As I realize that there's a lot of people reading your magazine, I thought I'd write to you about this delicate matter: common programming mistakes in REALbasic applications. There are a few.

1) Windows bigger than 640x480. These don't fit on the screens of older macs like mine (which is three years old...)
2) StaticText controls using 9 point system font. This is unreadable with many common system fonts like BeBox and OpenStep. If you need smaller text, use Geneva 9 or 10.

I've seen LOTS of RB-made apps doing these mistakes.

- Fabian Lidman

  Good points! Also, avoid putting system fonts in bold, as Charcoal and Chicago Bold look absolutely horrid.
