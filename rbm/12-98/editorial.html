Editor's Note

  First off, I'd like to explain why this issue came out 42 days after the previous one (not after one month, as the name "RB Monthly" might imply).

  The first two issues came out on the first day of the month, just like a normal magazine. Then, during the next month, I was unable to work on RBM for about two weeks, and spent some time trying to get other people to write for the magazine. This ended up making issue 3 come out a bit over a week late.

  After that, issues four and five ended up coming out a few days late each, which pushed the date for new issues to the 18th.

  At this point, I realized that I should take a break for the next twelve days, and restructure the magazine. Then, I could start coming out with new issues on December 1.

  So, that's why this issue was late: I simply wanted to get the release dates back on track again.

  And now, for the usual, "what's on my mind this month"...

  The new DR2 releases of RB are great! As anyone who used Realbasic during its DR1 period knows, the simple thrill of getting great new features once a week is wonderful. As RB approached 1.0, we saw a period of increasing stablility, but very few new features. As much as I want improved stability (RB crashes on me about once every 10 minutes), I have to admit, this was boring!

  However, the DR2 releases have one neat feature that the DR1 releases didn't: an aura of mystery.

  Have I gone psyho to think this? Not really. Downloading a new version of RB w/o knowing what you'll be getting is quite exciting—especially since you'll find out in a day or two.

  Also, the new features that are being added in the DR2s are also great—database access, QuickTime (which the cover story is on this month), more controls, navigation services, constants—the list goes on and on.

  When RB version 2.0 is released (or RB Pro, whichever DR2 becomes), it'll be one heck of an app. I'll certainly miss the mystery when it comes with release ntoes and a reference, but hey, practicality will ultimately win out. And if I get bored of that, there will certainly be a DR3.

  Just a thought...
- Dan Vanderkam
