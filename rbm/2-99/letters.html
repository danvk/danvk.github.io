  Just thought I'd say first off that I enjoy Rb Monthly, particularly this latest issue, with the dr2 stuff. I use dr2 all the time, haven't started up r1 since I got dr2, and am grateful whenever someone can reveal a little more of it's more or less hidden secrets.

  Anyway, I must take issue with your thoughts on Rb and boxes :-). I personally don't feel Rb should come in a box, and feel boxes are becoming more and more unnecessary as electronic distribution becomes more viable. For sure I feel it is important to have a physical copy of the software, but this can easily be accomplished by backing it up. Boxes inevitably make things more expensive, it's just the way it is. Marketing, shelf space, printing, pressing CD's etc. don't come for free and all would only increase the price. Being from New Zealand, boxless software is also welcome, as it means that we can obtain the software over the net without paying a premium for freight charges etc. Of course not all software is suitable for electronic distribution, it is too
large or whatever. Rb is relatively small though, so it is ideal for this form of distribution. CodeWarrior may come in a box, but it is infinitely more expensive, some $600 here, and it comes on 2 CD's, a Tools and a Reference CD. It would also be a formidable download. FWIW, CodeWarrior only comes with a brief "Getting Started" manual, all other documentation is on the Reference CD, in several formats (all roughly as bad as the others )

   I suppose you have a point there. Then again, making it come in a box would allow it to be sold through a variety of other sources, such as mail-order catalogs.

  I agree with you that the move away from paper manuals is a bad one, and I don't particularly like PDFs either. However there is the option of buying the manuals if you really want them. They are pretty expensive, but better that they be made available separately than included, perhaps in a box, and pushing the price up even more (not to mention shipping!). I would have bought the CD, and perhaps the manuals, but the shipping was almost more than the products themselves. It would have worked out cheaper to download the manuals as PDFs and have them printed out (although perhaps the printed manuals are different to the PDF ones, which seem very scanty and not really worth paying for, especially if you already have some programming experience). I do quite like the online help though, apart from it being a little slow.

  Still, something simple (like a Getting Started manual) would be quite inexpensive, and it would make RB seem more like a commercial product, and less like shareware.

  I totally agree with you about the keyboard shortcuts. Rb *needs* more, adding methods, properties etc. is a real chore. If RB registers with the Appearance Manager when it starts up and uses Appearance Manager calls to handle menus, which I assume it does, then you should be able
to add extended key shortcuts yourself using ResEdit. I've done this with a few programs I use regularly, so the shortcuts are consistent. By extended key shortcuts I mean ones like cmd-opt or cmd-shift. Most of the good shortcuts are already taken by the HI Guidelines :-), cmd-P would be great for New Property, but it is of course Print. cmd-opt-P would be ideal though. Of course, if you did want to change cmd-P to New Property, nothing is stopping you from changing it with ResEdit, and it is in fact very simple to do and should work with Rb.

  At least they've been added now. Problem is, I've always made the shortcut for New Property... Cmd-Opt-V, so I'm STILL not happy! (It stands for Variable, which is what that menu SHOULD read...).

To sum up, I think the distribution of Rb should stay how it is, except perhaps for people like yourself who can order a box and be happy when the shiny new package arrives in the mail :-). I personally don't need a box, I have too many old software boxes lying around as it is!

- Jo Meder
