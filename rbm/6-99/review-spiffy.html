  In the world of images, there are two main types: Bitmap, and Vector. Bitmaps are the ones we usually think of: A series of pixels. Programs like Photoshop edit these. But the other type of image is much more useful in many ways. It can generate smaller files, look as good as a printer can possibly print it (no matter what the DPI), and actual shapes in them can be edited–even after they're drawn.

  Creating a program that can edit vector images however, is far from simple. To spare you the difficulty of writing such a program, Jim Rodovich has created SpiffyVector.

  SpiffyVector provides all the basics of a vector image editor: polygons, fills, transparency, selections, rezizing, etc. The SpiffyVector class also goes beyond this, providing the ability to rotate polygons and include text in the graphic (this ability is quite limited though).

  
 Pros: Provides easy-to-use vector image support. Allows undoing. Allows different fill and border colors. Lets you save info to a string.
 Cons: Can't render to any picture at a scaled size. Selections are too strict. Must save colors and points seperately. No Beziers.
