Editor's Note

  The main highlight for RB this month has certainly been the DR2 releases. Thankfully, they now come with release notes, but still don't have an updated reference. However, this is something that I can understand much more easily than not bundling neccesary info that already exists. And, to make matters better, REAL SW already has a tech writer working on the updated reference. Hooray!

  Now, for my standard opinion-of-the-month™. Actually, this is a special issue! I have TWO opinions this month!

  When I purchased RB, I was rather dissapointed. When it was in its DR1 phase, I fully expected it to be a real commercial product–box and all. Even if it was sold only through catalogs and the realsoftware.com site, that would have been great. After all, Code Warrior comes in a box!

  It also reflects a trend in the computer industry that I find highly alarming–the lack of real manuals. Sure, programs come with a PDF or HTML guide, but that's a real pain. A real printed manual has no substitute, at least not in my opinion. Sitting down in a comfortable chair and reading sections of a manual is always fun and enlightening. At least it is sometimes. But still, I can guarantee you, it's no fun to sit at a computer and read a PDF. That's an eye-straining, slow, and ugly read. RB should have a box. It really should.

  Also, I'm beginning to develop the opinion that Andrew hates keyboard shortcuts. After all, RB has as few as is possible. Things like the "New Property" and "New Method" menu items don't have shortcuts, even though giving them ones saves you TONS of time. Trust me on this one, I know. And for the ultimate example, run a program. Click on the Variables window. Cmd-W doesn't even work now!

  What do I think shouldbe the case for keyboard shortcuts? I think that everything should have a shortcut. Everything. Or, even better, there could be a system like that in Microsoft Word (hey, 5.1 isn't bad!) where you can set a keyboard shortcut for anything.

  Just a few thoughts.
- Dan Vanderkam
